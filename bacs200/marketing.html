Our Mission

More than 800 children under age 5 die every day from diarrhea caused by contaminated water, poor sanitation, and unsafe hygiene. But we believe the global water and sanitation crisis can be solved within our lifetimes. That’s why we’re focused on ensuring clean water, sanitation, and hygiene (WASH) for every person in every community we work in, including the most vulnerable populations in the hardest-to-reach places. This work is more important now than ever, since clean water is essential for handwashing and disinfection to prevent the spread of diseases like COVID-19.

About World Vision

We are a Christian humanitarian organization. We partner with children, families, and their communities to reach their full potential by tackling the causes of poverty and injustice.

How We Do It

World Vision is committed to serving those in the most vulnerable countries, including those affected by extreme poverty and political instability. World Vision is committed to a deeper focus on serving the most vulnerable children in every country we are present in.

World Vision is dedicated not only to giving vulnerable populations to tools to access clean water, but also to teaching hygiene behaviors. This is especially important in preventing the spread of disease and saving lives

Statistics

  • 8.2 million people supported by taching hygiene behavior.
  • 2.7 million households constructed latrines for improved sanitation.
  • 3.4 million people provided with access to clean water.
